Assessment Methodology

This is an AI-estimated assessment performed using Claude Opus 4.6 (Anthropic), not a certified examination. The assessment was conducted through natural conversation, not prompted examination — the subject's authentic writing across diverse real-world contexts was the primary evidence base. No formal test conditions were administered.

  • Written corpus: ~20+ multi-turn conversations with Claude (Anthropic) covering technical, personal, commercial, and linguistic topics — December 2025 to February 2026
  • Email corpus: Sent emails in English over the past 2 years — service inquiries, customer support exchanges, professional correspondence
  • Prior analysis: A comprehensive communication style profile previously built by both Claude (Anthropic) and ChatGPT (OpenAI) independently, then merged
  • Listening evidence: Self-reported — decades of unsubtitled English media consumption (film, TV, news, video games, late night talk shows); confirmed full comprehension during international travel (Manhattan, early February 2026)
  • Speaking evidence: Self-reported — fluent internal formulation with output constrained by limited oral practice, introvert disposition, and noticeable French accent requiring initial listener adjustment
  • Benchmark data: Published corporate English requirements from Air France, Rakuten, Samsung, Sony; university admissions thresholds from Harvard, Oxford, MIT, Cambridge; French national TOEIC average
  • Exclusions: Intentional stylistic choices (lowercase typing, French-style punctuation spacing) and obvious keyboard typos were identified and excluded from scoring
  • Score calibration: Evaluator and subject collaborated on interpreting findings — the subject challenged assumptions and provided additional context, and the evaluator adjusted scores upward where evidence supported it, prioritizing honesty over conservatism

Scores should be treated as informed estimates. For certified results, a formal examination (IELTS, TOEFL, TOEIC, Cambridge) is recommended.
